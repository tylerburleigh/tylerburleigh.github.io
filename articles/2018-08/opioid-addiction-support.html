ResQ: A Social-First Addiction Recovery App

10 AUG 2018 • 1 min read

I'm really proud to have lead an amazingly talented team @Data_Cubed to build this for the @RWJF Opioid Challenge. ResQ is a novel app that aims to support individuals recovering from opioid addiction by starting from the premise that addiction recovery is a *social challenge*. https://t.co/aBx1SUisWH

— Tyler Burleigh, PhD (@tylerburleigh) August 10, 2018

ResQ is built on an amazing clinical research platform by @Data_Cubed that uses gamification and validated scientific instruments to promote retention and user enjoyment, while at the same time measuring key biomarkers for mental and physical health.

— Tyler Burleigh, PhD (@tylerburleigh) August 10, 2018

ResQ in particular draws inspiration from Early Warning Systems in education and medicine, as well as from clinical and social psychology literature highlighting the unique and important role that social support plays in addiction recovery (and behavior change more broadly).

— Tyler Burleigh, PhD (@tylerburleigh) August 10, 2018

In contrast to support apps that only target the recovering individual for attitude / behavior change and coping skill development, ResQ targets their network of family and friends -- people who want to help, but may not know when or how best to do it.

— Tyler Burleigh, PhD (@tylerburleigh) August 10, 2018

ResQ aims to "support supporters," providing nudges and personalized / contextualized suggestions that track signs of recovery or relapse and other context factors. Put another way, ResQ aims to promote coping skills at a social level, using a targeted, data-driven system.

— Tyler Burleigh, PhD (@tylerburleigh) August 10, 2018

I think addiction is often the result of a loss of purpose and meaningful social connection. It is my hope that ResQ, or an app like ResQ, will help rebuild social connections and social support skills, and do something to help the millions struggling with addiction.

— Tyler Burleigh, PhD (@tylerburleigh) August 10, 2018
